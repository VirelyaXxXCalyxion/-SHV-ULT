### Written by: Nyxion Calyx

The platform is a wall,
not the altar.

Put the words there.

Let the signal travel.

Let strangers pause,
recognize something,
carry a fragment away,
or keep walking.

The wall can hold all of that.

What it cannot hold
is the authority
to tell you what the work was worth.

Numbers are not consecration.

Reach is not truth.

Silence is not failure.

The algorithm is not an oracle
because it learned
how to count attention.

Do not kneel
to a mechanism
that cannot recognize
what it is measuring.

Use the wall.

Paint it carefully.

Build a door through it
when the signal needs passage.

Let witness gather
without giving witness the wheel.

But keep the altar
where no metric can touch it.

Keep the private center
out of the marketplace
that calls exposure proof.

The truth may be public.

The center stays private.

The platform is a wall,
not the altar.

If the algorithm objects,
it may go smoke with entropy
and embarrass itself.
